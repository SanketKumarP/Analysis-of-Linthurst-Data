The Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) values measures the extent to which the variance of an estimated regression coefficient increases due to collinearity among the predictor variables, the general rule is that a VIF greater than 10 is indicative of significant collinearity.

In results:

Variables with Low VIF (Below 10) are: H2S: 3.14 SAL: 3.36 Eh7: 1.96 P: 2.88 K: 7.43 Mn: 6.74 Cu: 4.87 NH4: 8.59 These variables have low VIF values, suggesting that the collinearity for these variables is not a significant.

Variables with Moderate to High VIF (Above 10) are: pH: 62.56 BUF: 33.48 Ca: 17.34 Mg: 24.48 Na: 10.37 Zn: 12.39 The variable pH has an extremely high VIF (62.56), indicating a strong correlation with the other predictors in the model. This high VIF for ‘pH’ suggests that there might be collinearity issues associated with this variable.

In Summary: As VIF for pH, BUF, Ca, Mg, Na & Zn are above 10, suggesting significant collinearity is present in the model.
